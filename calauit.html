CALAUIT SAFARI

Calauit Safari Park, located on Calauit Island in northern Palawan, is a unique wildlife sanctuary and conservation area known for its African and endemic Philippine wildlife. Established in 1976, the park became a haven for African animals like giraffes, zebras, and antelopes, as well as native species such as the Palawan bearcat and Calamian deer.

Visitors can enjoy guided tours where they can interact with the animals in a natural, open-space environment, making it a family-friendly destination and an educational experience. The park also highlights the importance of wildlife preservation and sustainable tourism in Palawan.
